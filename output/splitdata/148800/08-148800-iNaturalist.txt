The White-striped Longtail Skipper (according to Cirrus Image’s brief entry for this butterfly), “like many of the larger subtropical skippers, wander far north of their permanent range. Many lay eggs on these jaunts, but the butterflies remain resident only where warm weather permits; they survive winters only in South Texas and southward.” This butterfly favors many legumes, Mimosa and beans as host plants. The White-striped Longtail Skipper has several broods per year and is found year-round in South Texas. Its range includes South Texas and Arizona and thence south across the long length of the Western Hemisphere to Argentina. It is also native to the West Indies in the Caribbean.

Because it is a widespread butterfly of North America with its northernmost range occurring in Arizona and Texas in the United States, Mexico, and throughout the length of Central and South America claiming Argentina as its southernmost range, and including the Caribbean, the White-striped Longtail Skipper is an authentic resident of the Western Hemisphere.
